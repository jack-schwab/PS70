# pipe robot
    
# ---------------------------------

Fall 2024 PS70 had a unique machine building week. You see, someone clogged the Science Center toilet, and we had to fix it.

SC102 had an ultra-convenient bathroom... for about a week. The pipes got clogged thanks to, I'm assuming, Nick Ige. The Science Center's maintenance team was ready to blow $10,000 on a pipe inspection robot to locate the clog, so they can drill and clear it. That is, until Nathan stepped in.

The upshot: our class tried to build a pipe inspection robot. Unfortunately, I do not have any pictures of it. More unfortunately, the pipe robot never made its way down the tubes... yet!

My contribution to the whole project was to research ways to locate the robot once it went into the abyss. This sounds easy, until you realize it will be encased by a thick steel pipe under lots of concrete and Earth. There were two leading ideas: encoders to track the motor steps or TX/RX wireless communication.

Evan Jolley and I were on the TX/RX Team. Our catchphrase was "Beat the Crete", thus we both eventually became known as "Team Crete." We poured blood, sweat, and tears into our research, and I now consider Evan a brother to me.
