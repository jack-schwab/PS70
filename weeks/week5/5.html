# 3D design
    
# ---------------------------------

This week was a clear opportunity to make some progress on my final project. Despite that, I did not use this week to make any progress on my final project. Instead, I waited 10 weeks to 3D model and print parts for my final project.

My final project had two main 3D printed parts. The component housing

and DC Motor crank.

The housing is very simple, or, as Nathan would say, "crude." It is a handheld circular case with a hole cutout to attach the crank arm to the motor and another hole cutout to connect the power to the ESP32. The crank arm is also simple. I hand screwed a cap onto the free-end of the crank so that you can grip the cap and rotate the crank with ease (like a fishing reel).

The G-code files can be found here for the enclosure and here for the motor crank.
